More Info About The "Mini Rover" Project

Base

The base is connected at all times to a laptop, where the further processing of the incoming data takes place.
On the one hand the base receives the data from the rover before forwarding them to the processing unit, and on the other hand it transmits user instructions back to the rover.
So the base constitutes the intermediate link between our "data collector" and our "data processor".
Motion instructions could be given by the user through the Joystick or the Microphone.

The base consists of the following elements:

  • Printed Circuit Board
  • Arduino Nano
  • IR Emitter
  • RF Transmitter
  • RF Receiver
  • Microphone
  • Temperature Sensor
  • Magnetic Field Detector
  • Buzzer
  • RGB Led
  • Touch Detector
  • Button
  • Joystick


Mini Rover

The mini rover is equipped with a variety of sensors which gather information about the environmental conditions. Through the RF modules the data are sent back to the base.
At the first stage of the development, the rover's movement is indicated by lines on the track, which are detected by the corresponding sensor.
At the second stage, the rover could be guided by an AI system whose inputs are the distance measurements of surrounding objects.
The rover consists of the following elements:

  • 2xPrinted Circuit Board
  • 2xArduino Nano
  • 2xDC Motors + Motor Driver Circuit
  • IR Receiver
  • RF Transmitter/Receiver
  • SD Card Module
  • LCD Display
  • Microphone
  • Humidity/Temperature Sensor
  • Magnetic Field Sensor
  • Flame Detector
  • 2xTilt Detectors
  • Vibration Detector
  • Buzzer
  • RGB SMD
  • Photoresistor
  • Line Tracker
  • 2xDistance Sensors
